Obsidian
Form Follows Feeling
I — Form

Geometry so considered, you'll reach for it just to hold it.

Every radius is intentional. Every surface tension is calculated. Obsidian does not copy the category — it quietly redefines what a rectangle can feel like when it lives in your hand. Tapered edges that vanish. Corners that meet the light at angles we spent two years getting right.

II — Material

Cold-forged volcanic glass,
set in aerospace alloy.

The name is not metaphor. The rear panel is formed from a proprietary crystalline glass process that renders the surface with the deep, non-reflective stillness of volcanic stone. It doesn't gleam. It absorbs light. It invites touch. Set against a frame milled from a single billet of 7000-series aluminium, this is not a manufactured object — it is a found object, refined.

III — Colour

Colours chosen by a painter.
Not a focus group.

Each colourway is a complete emotional statement. Obsidian (the deepest black we have ever seen in glass). Dusk (a near-invisible violet that only reveals itself in certain light). Sand (warm, quiet, like dried earth after rain). And Ash — for those who want nothing between them and the object itself.
